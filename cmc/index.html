Classical Mathematical Community

This website is a place for us to work out how to teach mathematics classically and Chritianly. The thesis of the website is that treating mathematics as one of her peer humanities is the most direct path towards that goal. Dave Mathwin, history teacher at Ad Fontes, says that religion is upstream of culture, culture is upstream of politics, and politics upstream of military results. So if you want to understand why a battle unfolds the way it unfolds, look first to the religious beliefs of the societies involved. Analagously, I'd propose this stream for the mathematics classroom:

Religion is upstream of culture; culture upstream of pedagogy; and pedagogy upstream of materials, and materials upstream of assessment.

If you have the right materials but the wrong culture, your classroom will not thrive. If you have the right culture, the right materials will flow from that.

Consequently, this website follows that division. It has a page about the relationship between Christ an mathematics, a page about forming the culture of our mathematics classrooms, a page about good materials, and a page about how to assess student growth. I'd encourage you to look through them in that order -- the materials and assessments don't make a lot of sense absent the cultural framework that animates them.
